First Plus - Money Laundering Legislation, Requirements & Reporting 
As a First Plus Money Transfer (FPMT) Payment System Agent, you must report any suspicious or unusual transactions to the First Plus Money Laundering Officer. The MLRO will make his own judgments and pass on any appropriate information to the relevant Law Enforcement Agencies. 
The UK’s Money Laundering Regulations came in to force in March 2004 these regulations now ensure established reporting and record keeping procedures for UK relevant financial services, such as First Plus. These regulations also apply to First Plus third party Agents who are therefore required to assist in the prevention and detection of possible money laundering activities. 
The First Plus Training manual is intended to assist you in understanding these laws and to help you in complying with them. Any aspect of the regulations in this manual that you do not fully understand then please do not hesitate to contact the Money Laundering officer who will be happy to assist you. It also requires First Plus to monitor and report on any unusual or suspicious foreign currency transactions of any size.

 Please remember that the law affects YOU too. If you or your staff knowingly fails to report an incident or deliberately helps (e.g. by breaking down the transaction amounts) this is known as 'willful blindness' and is a legal principle that has previously been used in successful convictions. Bear this in mind with particular reference to potentially 'suspicious' transactions. 

The Law also makes it an offence for anyone to provide assistance to a criminal so as to obtain, conceals invest or transfer funds, if this is done knowingly or in suspicious circumstances. 

Such assistance is punishable on conviction by a maximum of 14 years imprisonment or a fine, or both. 
It is also an offence for anyone to take action likely to prejudice an investigation by tipping off or warning a person who is the subject of a suspicious transaction, or where a disclosure has been made and Customs & Excise or Police are investigating money laundering activities. 
Such 'tipping off' is punishable on conviction by a maximum of 5 years imprisonment or a fine or both. 
The Law requires us to maintain full and detailed records of all 'Large Transactions' or of 'Suspicious Transactions' with copies of any identification supplied by the customer. These are retained for a minimum of 5 years following the transaction. 
Acceptable IDs are: valid passport, driving license with photo or country ID document, recent (within previous three months) credit / debit card statement, utility bill. We are unable to process any transaction where this information is withheld. 
Signs of Suspicious Transactions: 
  1. Tattered Notes
  2. High numbers of notes
  3. Large number of mixed bundles of notes
  4. Lack of interest in exchange rates
  5. Customer appearing inconsistent with type or size of transaction
  6. Transactions at normally quiet periods (e.g. just before closing)
  7. Hurried transaction 
  8. Regular customer
  9. Splitting transactions (e.g. to avoid crossing a 'Large Transaction limit) 
  10. Failure to divulge information for a Large Transaction 
  11. Enquiries as to reporting procedures 
  12. any other circumstances giving reasonable cause for suspicion 
The above list is not exhaustive, merely a guide. It is the legal responsibility of each individual member of your staff to make their own judgment. 

Reporting Procedure:- 
  1. DO NOT alert the customer 
  2. DO NOT inconvenience genuine customers 
  3. Have the customer complete a 'Large Transaction' Form 
  4. Obtain valid ID, noting the customer's name and details (take a copy) 
  5. Fax this to the First Plus Authorization Centre clearly marked "FPMLO/LTF/ID". (first plus money laundering officer/ large transaction form/ identification) Fax: 084502572148. Then please call: 0845 2572147 to confirm that your fax has been received 
  6. Act according to instructions from First Plus 
  7. Failure to provide adequate ID or to complete the Large Transaction Form - DO NOT PROCEED WITH THE TRANSACTION 
  8. Ensure that all details of the transaction are carefully recorded 
  9. Make any notes immediately following the customer's departure 
REPORT the incident directly afterwards to the First Plus Money laundering Officer by calling 0845 2572147 
In case of any query, please contact the First Plus Money Laundering Officer for advice
Possible excuses which customers try to avoid providing ID
  • Introduction by an established customer or local shop
  • Lost or stolen ID
Special notes: 
  • Senders must present an acceptable document(s) showing evidence of identity for amounts over £600.00. 
  • For amounts of £1,000 and over, senders must present 2 forms of identification, 1. Photo ID and 2. must be proof of address (within previous 3 months)
