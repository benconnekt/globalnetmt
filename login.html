GlobalNet Payment System - Agent Information


Money Laundering Legislation, Requirements & Reporting

UK law has established reporting and record keeping procedures for UK money transfer businesses, such as GlobalNet. These regulations also apply to GlobalNet third party Agents who are therefore required to assist in the prevention and detection of possible money laundering activities.

The GlobalNet Compliant manual is intended to assist you in understanding these laws and to help you in complying with them. It also requires GlobalNet to monitor and report on any unusual or suspicious foreign currency transactions of any size.

As a GlobalNet Payment System Agent, you must report any suspicious or unusual transactions to the GlobalNet Money Laundering Officer. The MLRO will make his own judgements and pass on any appropriate information to the relevant Law Enforcement Agencies.

Please remember that the law affects YOU too. If you or your staffs knowingly fail to report an incident or deliberately help (e.g. by breaking down the transaction amounts) this is known as 'wilful blindness' and is a legal principle that has previously been used in successful convictions. Bear this in mind with particular reference to potentially 'suspicious' transactions.

The Law also makes it an offence for anyone to provide assistance to a criminal so as to obtain, conceals invest or transfer funds, if this is done knowingly or in suspicious circumstances.

Such assistance is punishable on conviction by a maximum of 14 years imprisonment or a fine, or both.

It is also an offence for anyone to take action likely to prejudice an investigation by tipping off or warning a person who is the subject of a suspicious transaction, or where a disclosure has been made and Customs & Revenue or Police are investigating money laundering activities.
Such 'tipping off' is punishable on conviction by a maximum of 5 years imprisonment or a fine or both.

The Law requires us to maintain full and detailed records of all 'Large Transactions' or of 'Suspicious Transactions' with copies of any identification supplied by the customer. These are retained for a minimum of 5 years following the transaction. Acceptable IDs are: valid passport, driving licence with photo or country ID document.


Signs of Suspicious Transactions:-

Tattered Notes

High numbers of notes

Large number of mixed bundles of notes

Lack of interest in exchange rates

Customer appearing inconsistent with type or size of transaction

Transactions at normally quiet periods (eg just before closing)

Hurried transaction

Regular customer

Splitting transactions (e.g. to avoid crossing a 'Large Transaction limit)

Failure to divulge information for a Large Transaction

Enquiries as to reporting procedures

Any other circumstances giving reasonable cause for suspicion

The above list is not exhaustive, merely a guide. It is the legal responsibility of each individual member of your staff to make their own judgement.


Reporting Procedure:-

DO NOT alert the customer
DO NOT inconvenience genuine customers
Have the customer complete a 'Large Transaction' Form
Obtain valid ID, noting the customer's name and details (take a copy)
Fax this to the GlobalNet Authorisation Centre clearly marked "hold/ID". Fax: 020 81816629. If no response after 5 minutes, call by phone to check.
Act according to instructions from GlobalNet.
Failure to provide adequate ID or to complete the Large Transaction Form - DO NOT PROCEED WITH THE TRANSACTION
Ensure that all details of the transaction are carefully recorded
Make any notes immediately following the customer's departure
REPORT the incident directly afterwards to the GlobalNet Money laundering Officer Tel: 02083187496 for UK

In case of any query, please contact the GlobalNet Money Laundering Officer for advice.
